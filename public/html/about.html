Hi, Im Oakley.

I would consider myself a logical person, always trying to understand why things are, although I enjoy time spent with animals and outside more than anything.
I dont find it easy to write about who I am, sounds pretentious to me, so I thought the best way to do an about page, is to show what made me, me.

Before I felt old?

Throughout early school life, I remember myself excelling in science, math, and history. Not as much with reading, I used to go through the same handful of simple books until I finally figured out the difference between non-fiction and fiction.
Before my GCSEs, I was less confident in maths, but I still loved science. The numbers started to confuse me, but the patterns in life, biology especially, is still fascinating to me.
For GCSES, i took triple science, history, graphic products, and computer science. I picked triple science for the chemistry and biology, physics to me is just maths by applied, atleast in school. Space has always been incredible to wonder about, but its barely touched on when you are learning "physics". History was a passion pick, Im not amazing with dates, but I love the progression of politics, society, and human understanding, our species has some crazy stories. Computer science I chose because its what I was fairly adequate at, I understood hardware, I knew how the internet worked, and I was learning a few programming languages at the time.

Solo sports are best

I've never been one for traditional team sports, while I like teamwork, I prefer to be fully dependable for my success or falls. Im also terrible at anything with a ball, I really have no idea why, my coordination is pretty good but something about an inflated skin just doesnt click.

Snowboarding is just a win/win of a sport. You get to exercise in the cold which is comfortable, you usually have some padding when you fall over, the views are incredible, and you get to zoom. There really isnt anything as gratifying, no matter what it seems to be a good season. Saying that, its been a while since ive been, not much snow in Falmouth.
I wasnt great at Badminton, and then i grew. Safe to say, height is a cheat code in racket sports, and from playing often before COVID-19 I got pretty good, although its another sport Id like to get back into.
I also enjoy just exploring, going on long walks, using random outdoor gyms, cycling, and other random activities. They always act as good activities to break up the day and help me relax.

A and B levels.

Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science...

Enough of a workload where you want to complain at 16, but the Chem, Bio, Math students wont let you.

That being said, Biology was something I was pretty good at, I forgoed some of the maths, for more actually knowledge biased questions, the maths that was involved was often quite understandable, as I tied back to graspable concepts.
Computer science was kind of a breeze, It was a great class to chill and relearn some old material, learn some new bits, and code. I learnt alot of my web development skills in the same time frame, so it went hand in hand with my NEA, which you can see on the project page.
Chemistry was where I struggled, visualising and understanding the processes and particles was pretty understandable, but i couldnt then understand some of the more complicated formula, and interactions between the hundreds of thousands of possible molecule combinations. I wish it wasnt half online.
All in all, Alevels were a fairly decent period, although the COVID-19 pandemic did take a toll on my education, but I walked a way with a place in Uni, and Alevels in all 3.
